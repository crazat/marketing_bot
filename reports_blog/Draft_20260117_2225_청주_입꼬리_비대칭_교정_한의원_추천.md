### 1. Medical Fact: Physiological Causes
In Oriental Medicine, lip asymmetry is rarely viewed as a localized lip issue; it is seen as a symptom of structural and muscular imbalance.
*   **Muscular Imbalance:** Over-activity or shortening of the *risorius* and *zygomaticus* muscles on one side, often caused by unilateral chewing habits or side-sleeping.
*   **TMJ (Temporomandibular Joint) Disorder:** Misalignment of the jaw joint (턱관절) is the most common root cause. When the mandible shifts, the soft tissue (lips) follows the skeletal deviation.
*   **Cervical Spine (Neck) Misalignment:** The "Upper Cross Syndrome" or tilted C1-C2 vertebrae can cause the facial fascia to pull unevenly, resulting in a lopsided smile.
*   **Oriental Medicine Approach:** Focuses on **Myeonsang-chim (Facial Acupuncture)** to balance muscle tone and **Chuna Manual Therapy** to realign the jaw and neck.

### 2. Patient Pain Points: What They Worry About
Patients seeking correction in Cheongju typically express the following concerns:
*   **Photogenic Anxiety:** "My face looks crooked in selfies or ID photos," and "I look like I’m smirking or mocking someone when I smile."
*   **First Impression Stress:** Frequent worries that asymmetry makes them look "angry," "untrustworthy," or "gloomy" during job interviews or first dates.
*   **Fear of Invasive Surgery:** Many patients are terrified of "bone-cutting" (osteotomy) and are looking for non-surgical, natural alternatives like acupuncture or thread lifting.
*   **Functional Discomfort:** Pain in the jaw while eating, "clicking" sounds in the ear, or chronic tension headaches accompanying the asymmetry.
*   **Sustainability:** "Will my face go back to being crooked as soon as the treatment stops?" (Concerns about the root cause vs. temporary fix).
