    *   **Skin Boosters + Hydration:** Combining Rejuran with hyaluronic acid injections addresses both cellular repair and immediate moisture retention.
*   **The Goal:** These packages aim to treat multiple layers of the tissue (epidermis, dermis, and muscle) simultaneously for a more holistic result than a single treatment could provide.

### 2. Patient Pain Points: The "Cheap Price" Paradox
Patients interested in "Value-for-Money" (가성비) packages are often conflicted by the following anxieties:
*   **Quality Dilution:** Fear that "discounted" means the clinic is using diluted Botox (over-mixing with saline) or using counterfeit/low-quality tips for laser machines.
*   **The "Factory" Experience:** Worry that the consultation will be handled by a coordinator rather than a doctor, leading to a "one-size-fits-all" treatment that ignores their specific facial anatomy.
*   **Hidden Costs:** Concern that the advertised price is a "bait-and-switch" and that VAT (10%) or mandatory "post-op care" fees will be added at the desk.
*   **Overtreatment:** Anxiety that because it is a "package," they will receive procedures they don't actually need, potentially leading to side effects like "unnatural" frozen expressions or skin sensitivity.

*   **The "December Rush" (Health Screenings):** In Korea, the end of the year sees a massive spike in health check-up packages because the National Health Insurance (NHIS) cycle expires on December 31st.
*   **Graduation & Job Seeker Season (Jan–Feb):** A peak period for "Value Packages" targeting students. Trends show a high demand for "Petit" procedures (Botox/Filler) and acne scar treatments before the spring semester/hiring season.
*   **The "Holiday Recovery" Trend:** Following Chuseok or Seollal, there is a statistical increase in "Anti-stress" IV drip packages and fatigue recovery treatments for housewives and office workers.
*   **Platform-Driven Pricing:** With the rise of apps like *Gangnam Unni* and *Yeoshin Ticket*, price transparency in Korea has reached an all-time high. The trend is moving from "lowest price" to **"Best Value-for-Time" (시성비)**—packages that offer maximum results with minimum clinic visits.
